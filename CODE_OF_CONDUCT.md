# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and constructive environment for everyone who participates in this project.

Participation should be based on the quality of ideas, evidence, implementation, and collaboration—not on personal background, identity, experience level, employment, affiliation, or status.

This commitment applies to contributors, maintainers, reviewers, researchers, users, community members, and anyone representing the project.

## Expected behaviour

Participants are expected to:

* Communicate respectfully and professionally.
* Assume good faith while remaining willing to question claims and evidence.
* Critique ideas, designs, implementations, and results rather than individuals.
* Provide actionable, technically grounded feedback.
* Acknowledge uncertainty, limitations, and mistakes.
* Correct errors when they are identified.
* Respect differing levels of experience and familiarity with the project.
* Give appropriate credit for ideas, research, code, findings, and other contributions.
* Respect confidentiality when information has been shared privately.
* Follow project security and disclosure processes.
* Avoid disrupting discussions, reviews, or project operations.
* Help maintain a community where disagreement can remain rigorous without becoming hostile.

Technical disagreement is expected and often valuable. Strong criticism of a design, claim, benchmark, protocol mechanism, or implementation is acceptable when it is relevant, evidence-based, and expressed respectfully.

## Unacceptable behaviour

Unacceptable behaviour includes:

* Harassment, intimidation, threats, or personal attacks.
* Discriminatory, demeaning, or derogatory comments.
* Unwelcome sexual attention or sexualised language.
* Deliberate misgendering or use of rejected personal identifiers.
* Publishing another person’s private information without permission.
* Revealing confidential reports, credentials, contact details, or security-sensitive information.
* Sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, events, or project infrastructure.
* Repeatedly ignoring reasonable requests to stop inappropriate behaviour.
* Trolling, baiting, or deliberately escalating conflict.
* Impersonating contributors, maintainers, organisations, runners, or other project participants.
* Falsifying evidence, results, attestations, authorship, provenance, or contribution history.
* Knowingly misrepresenting proposed behaviour as deployed or verified behaviour.
* Retaliating against someone who reports a concern or participates in an investigation.
* Encouraging or assisting others to engage in prohibited behaviour.

Good-faith mistakes, incorrect technical claims, failed experiments, and disputed interpretations are not misconduct by themselves. Problems should ordinarily be addressed through correction, evidence, review, and discussion unless there is deception, harassment, repeated bad faith, or another serious breach.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including:

* Source repositories and pull requests.
* Issues and GitHub Discussions.
* Code review and design review.
* Project documentation and research artifacts.
* Benchmark, scenario, evidence, and attestation workflows.
* Chat rooms, forums, mailing lists, and community calls.
* Conferences, demonstrations, workshops, and project events.
* Private communication related to project work.
* Public communication made while representing the project.

Behaviour outside project spaces may also be considered when it creates a credible risk to participants, materially affects the project, or undermines a person’s ability to participate safely.

## Project representation

A person may be considered to be representing the project when they:

* Use an official project account or communication channel.
* Speak on behalf of the project or its maintainers.
* Act in an assigned project role.
* Present project work at an event.
* Publish an official artifact, report, benchmark, attestation, or announcement.
* Are explicitly identified as a project representative.

Personal opinions should not be presented as official project positions without authorisation.

## Reporting a concern

Reports should be made privately to:

marc@intrinsic.global

Include `CODE OF CONDUCT` in the subject line.

Do not report sensitive conduct concerns through public issues, discussions, or pull requests.

A report should include, where possible:

* A description of what occurred.
* When and where it occurred.
* Relevant links, messages, screenshots, or other evidence.
* The names or identifiers of the people involved.
* Whether the behaviour is continuing.
* Any immediate safety or confidentiality concerns.
* The outcome or support being requested.

Reports may be submitted by a person directly affected or by a witness.

If the report concerns the person responsible for receiving conduct reports, use an alternative maintainer contact or another reporting route identified by the project.

Security vulnerabilities, leaked credentials, and evidence-integrity failures should follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). A report involving both security and conduct concerns may be submitted through either private route, with a note that both policies may apply.

## Privacy and confidentiality

Maintainers will handle reports as privately as reasonably possible.

Information may be shared with people who need it to assess the report, protect participants, obtain legal or professional advice, or implement an appropriate response.

The project will not intentionally publish the identity of a reporter or affected person without permission, except where disclosure is legally required or necessary to address an immediate risk.

Absolute confidentiality cannot be guaranteed.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Maintainers responsible for enforcement are expected to:

* Review reports impartially and in good faith.
* Avoid conflicts of interest.
* Protect reporters and participants from retaliation.
* Consider context, severity, intent, impact, repetition, and prior behaviour.
* Distinguish technical disagreement from personal misconduct.
* Apply responses consistently and proportionately.
* Document significant enforcement decisions privately.
* Correct or reconsider a decision when material new information becomes available.

A maintainer with a conflict of interest should withdraw from handling the report where practical.

## Enforcement process

After receiving a report, maintainers may:

1. Confirm receipt.
2. Assess immediate safety, security, or confidentiality risks.
3. Gather relevant information.
4. Give affected participants an opportunity to provide context where appropriate.
5. Determine whether this Code of Conduct was violated.
6. Apply a proportionate response.
7. Communicate the outcome to relevant parties where appropriate.
8. Record follow-up actions or conditions.

The project does not guarantee a particular response time or outcome. Urgent threats, exposed secrets, or active security risks may require immediate action before a full review is completed.

## Possible responses

Responses may include:

### Informal correction

A private explanation of the concern and a request to change the behaviour.

Appropriate for minor, isolated, or apparently unintentional conduct.

### Formal warning

A documented warning describing the unacceptable behaviour, required changes, and consequences of repetition.

### Contribution or communication restrictions

Temporary limits on participation in discussions, reviews, issues, pull requests, events, or other project spaces.

### Removal of content or privileges

Removal or reversal of comments, artifacts, roles, permissions, credentials, attestations, or project responsibilities where necessary.

### Temporary suspension

A time-limited exclusion from some or all project spaces.

### Permanent exclusion

Permanent removal from project spaces or roles for severe, repeated, threatening, deceptive, or retaliatory behaviour.

### Referral

Referral to a hosting provider, employer, event organiser, platform, law-enforcement body, or other relevant authority when necessary and appropriate.

Responses may be combined. More serious measures may be applied immediately when the conduct involves credible threats, harassment, deliberate evidence falsification, exposure of sensitive information, retaliation, or significant risk to participants or the project.

## Corrections and appeals

A person subject to a formal enforcement action may request reconsideration by providing:

* The decision being challenged.
* Relevant facts or context believed to have been missed.
* Any corrective action already taken.
* The outcome being requested.

Requests should be submitted privately through the conduct-reporting address.

Where practical, reconsideration should be handled by a maintainer who was not the primary decision-maker. Reconsideration does not guarantee reversal of the original decision.

## Retaliation

Retaliation against a person for making a good-faith report, supporting a reporter, providing evidence, or participating in an investigation is prohibited.

A report that is not substantiated is not necessarily a false or malicious report.

Knowingly false reports, fabricated evidence, or use of the reporting process to harass another participant may themselves violate this Code of Conduct.

## Research integrity

Because this project involves research, simulations, benchmarks, evidence, and protocol analysis, participants must not knowingly:

* Fabricate or alter results.
* Hide material assumptions or known limitations.
* Misstate what a scenario, benchmark, or test establishes.
* Present generated evidence as independently reproduced when it was not.
* Falsely claim authorship, review, approval, or attestation.
* Remove provenance or attribution in a misleading way.
* Represent proposed protocol behaviour as deployed Solidity parity.
* Suppress a material conflicting result while presenting a conclusion as comprehensive.

Disputes about methodology or interpretation should be addressed through evidence, reproducibility, review, and transparent documentation.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is informed by established open-source community standards, including the Contributor Covenant, while being adapted for this project’s technical, research, evidence, and protocol-model context.
